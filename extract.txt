Keywords:                                               encountering settings of lacking target labels and distinct feature variations, even the latest classification
Satellite image time series                             algorithms may perform poorly in transferring knowledge from a trained dataset to an unknown target
Unsupervised domain adaptation                          dataset, despite similar or even identical label sets. The research field of unsupervised domain adaptation
Transfer learning                                       (UDA) focuses on methods to overcome these challenges by providing algorithms that explicitly learn domain
Crop type mapping                                       shifts between different data domains in the absence of target-labeled data. Building upon recent advances on
Deep learning                                           generic UDA research in time series settings, we propose RAINCOAT-SRS, an enhancement of the frequency-
                                                        augmented UDA-algorithm RAINCOAT specifically for the SITS domain. To evaluate the default and adjusted
                                                        model variants, we designed several closed-label set, cross-regional and multi-temporal crop type mapping
                                                        experiments, which represent common sub-problems of UDA in SITS. We first benchmark RAINCOAT against
                                                        TimeMatch as a leading algorithm in this application context. Subsequently, we explored different encoder-
                                                        to-decoder constellations as architectural enhancements. These analyses revealed that a combination of an
                                                        self-attention-based encoder with the default decoder yields a performance increase to the standard algorithm
                                                        of up to 6 % in average f1-score, and to TimeMatch by up to 24 %. Beyond, we assessed the impact of the
                                                        frequency feature and SITS-specific feature extensions by integrating weather data, which both showed to
                                                        improve classification accuracy only in individual sub-experiments however not consistently across the entire
                                                        scope of scenarios. Finally, we outline key factors influencing the transferability, thereby emphasizing the
                                                        major importance of domain-overarching stability of class-relative, structural patterns rather than of collective,
                                                        linear shifts between domains. Through this research, we introduce RAINCOAT-SRS, a novel model for UDA
                                                        in SITS, designed to advance generalization in remote sensing by enabling more comprehensive cross-regional
                                                        and multi-temporal SITS experiments in face of lacking target-labeled data.

                                                                                                   To investigate the impact of geographic context knowledge, we
                                                                                               prepared weather data throughout the study area at a resolution of
                                                                                               NUTS 2-regional level. Level 2 corresponds hereby to the 3rd finest
                                                                                               level of administrative regions in the EU (European Union, 2023). For
                                                                                               each region a mean value has been calculated based on five geographic
                                                                                               locations: the area’s center point and four times the midpoint between
                                                                                               the center and the four corners of the area’s total bounds. For the
                                                                                               five locations, we derived for each day of the two considered years
                                                                                               2021 and 2022, data on three weather metrics including mean air
                                                                                               temperature, precipitation sum, and solar radiation sum per day. These
                                                                                               parameters show to be among the most influential weather variables
                                                                                               related to plant growth (Shook et al., 2021; van Bussel et al., 2016).
                                                                                               We retrieved the data using the free version of the Open-Meteo weather
                                                                                               API (Zippenfenig, 2023). Finally, the daily records of each sub-region
                                                                                               have been averaged on a 10-daily basis to reach the target sequence
                                                                                               length of 36 time steps. Mapping each parcel to its corresponding NUTS
                                                                                               2-region then establishes the simplified joining of the three weather
                                                                                               variables to a parcel’s 10-dimensional, multispectral sequences yielding
